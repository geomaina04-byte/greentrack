Privacy

Traceability without oversharing

A batch tag shares what a buyer needs to trust their food, and nothing more.

What's on a tag

Farm, date, treatment — never a person

A public batch tag shows the farm it came from, the harvest date, and its treatment history. It never shows a grower's personal details, exact home address, or financial data. Growers decide what's visible on a public tag before it's ever printed.

Anyone scanning a tag does so without creating an account, so there's nothing tying a shopper's, chef's, or diner's identity to the batches they check.

Farm data stays yoursLocation pins and yield history are private until you choose to publish a tag.
Tags carry only what's neededBatch, date, treatment status — nothing that identifies a person.
No account to scanAnyone verifying a tag can do so without signing up or being tracked.
You can delete your dataGrowers can remove a plot's history at any time; published tags are removed with it.
