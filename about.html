Why GreenTrack exists

One batch, four stops, zero guesswork

GreenTrack started as a way for growers to track their own plots. It became something bigger once we realised the same record could follow the harvest all the way to the plate.

Our story

Built from a simple frustration

It started with a grower who kept a paper notebook for every plot — planting dates, watering, which bed got which pesticide — and a shopper two towns away staring at a label that just said "fresh," with no way to check. GreenTrack closes that gap: one record, started the day a seed goes into the ground, that keeps collecting stamps until it reaches whoever's about to eat it.

One batch, four stops

Follow a single crate from plot to plate

Every stage adds a stamp to the same tag — nobody has to take anyone else's word for it.

STOP 01

The Grower

Mwangi, farmer

Pins his plot by GPS, logs every irrigation, and clears a pre-harvest pesticide countdown before the app lets him log the harvest at all.

STOP 02

The Shopper

Faith, buyer

Skeptical of a label that just says "fresh," she scans the tag in the aisle and sees the exact farm and the route her greens travelled.

STOP 03

The Chef

Sarah, restaurant

Instead of trusting a supplier's word, she scans the crate and confirms it was picked hours ago and carries organic certification.

STOP 04

The Diner

Mary, guest

A small tag by the menu turns into a full nutrient and freshness breakdown, logged straight into her fitness tracker.

Where it happens

Every stamp starts on the grower's phone

Plots, irrigation, pest checks, and the harvest lock all live in the same app a grower already opens every morning — the traceability is a side effect of doing their normal work.
